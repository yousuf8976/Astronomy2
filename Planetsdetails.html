PLANET DETAILS

Explore the fascinating details about the planets in our solar system!

Earth

Discovered: Earth was known to ancient civilizations and is the only planet known to support life. (No formal "discovery" as it's our home planet.)

Size: 12,742 km in diameter.

Atmosphere: Earth's atmosphere is composed of 78% nitrogen, 21% oxygen, and trace amounts of other gases.

Distance from the Sun: 149.6 million km (1 AU).

Distance from Earth: N/A (as it's our home planet).

Important Details: Earth is the only known planet with liquid water and the right conditions to support life. It has one natural satellite, the Moon, which affects tides and stabilizes the planet's axial tilt.

Mercury

Discovered: Mercury has been known since ancient times, but the first space probe to study it was NASA's Mariner 10, which flew by in 1974.

Size: 4,880 km in diameter.

Atmosphere: Mercury has a very thin atmosphere, composed mostly of oxygen, sodium, hydrogen, helium, and potassium.

Distance from the Sun: 57.91 million km (0.39 AU).

Distance from Earth: Varies between 77 million and 222 million km.

Important Details: Mercury has extreme temperature fluctuations due to its lack of atmosphere, ranging from -173°C at night to 427°C during the day. It has no moons.

Venus

Discovered: Venus has been known since ancient times. It was studied by NASA's Magellan spacecraft from 1990 to 1994.

Size: 12,104 km in diameter.

Atmosphere: Venus has a thick, toxic atmosphere made mostly of carbon dioxide, with clouds of sulfuric acid. It has an extremely strong greenhouse effect.

Distance from the Sun: 108.2 million km (0.72 AU).

Distance from Earth: Varies between 38 million and 261 million km.

Important Details: Venus is known as Earth's "sister planet" due to its similar size, but its surface temperature can reach up to 465°C, hotter than Mercury's despite being further from the Sun. Venus rotates in the opposite direction of most planets in the solar system.

Mars

Discovered: Mars has been known since ancient times. It was visited by NASA's Viking spacecraft in 1976 and more recently by rovers like Curiosity and Perseverance.

Size: 6,779 km in diameter.

Atmosphere: Mars has a thin atmosphere made primarily of carbon dioxide (95%), with nitrogen and argon.

Distance from the Sun: 227.9 million km (1.52 AU).

Distance from Earth: Varies between 54.6 million and 401 million km.

Important Details: Mars is often called the "Red Planet" due to its iron oxide-rich surface. It has two moons: Phobos and Deimos. Mars is a prime candidate for future human exploration.

Jupiter

Discovered: Jupiter has been known since ancient times, but its first close-up study was by NASA's Pioneer 10 in 1973.

Size: 139,820 km in diameter, the largest planet in the solar system.

Atmosphere: Jupiter's atmosphere is mostly hydrogen and helium, with traces of methane, water vapor, and ammonia.

Distance from the Sun: 778.5 million km (5.2 AU).

Distance from Earth: Varies between 588 million and 968 million km.

Important Details: Jupiter has at least 79 moons, including Ganymede, the largest moon in the solar system. It also has a massive storm system known as the Great Red Spot.

Saturn

Discovered: Saturn has been known since ancient times. It was first observed in detail by Galileo in 1610.

Size: 116,460 km in diameter.

Atmosphere: Saturn's atmosphere is mostly hydrogen and helium, with traces of methane, ammonia, and water.

Distance from the Sun: 1.43 billion km (9.58 AU).

Distance from Earth: Varies between 1.2 billion and 1.7 billion km.

Important Details: Saturn is known for its stunning ring system, made of ice and rock particles. It has 82 known moons, including Titan, the second-largest moon in the solar system.

Uranus

Discovered: Uranus was discovered by Sir William Herschel in 1781.

Size: 50,724 km in diameter.

Atmosphere: Uranus has an atmosphere composed mainly of hydrogen, helium, and methane, which gives it a blue-green color.

Distance from the Sun: 2.87 billion km (19.2 AU).

Distance from Earth: Varies between 2.6 billion and 3.0 billion km.

Important Details: Uranus has a tilted axis, which causes extreme seasonal variations. It has 27 known moons, with Miranda being the most geologically active.

Neptune

Discovered: Neptune was discovered by Johann Galle in 1846 based on mathematical predictions.

Size: 49,244 km in diameter.

Atmosphere: Neptune's atmosphere is composed mostly of hydrogen, helium, and methane, with some ammonia and water.

Distance from the Sun: 4.5 billion km (30.07 AU).

Distance from Earth: Varies between 4.3 billion and 4.7 billion km.

Important Details: Neptune is known for its strong winds, the fastest in the solar system. It has 14 known moons, with Triton being the largest and geologically active.
